III. Types of Data Collected

At Web-Impuls, we collect various types of personal data to provide our web development, design, and promotion services effectively. This includes:

  • Personal identification information: your full name, email address, phone number, and postal address, which you provide when contacting us, placing an order, or signing up for our newsletter.
  • Billing information: payment details such as credit card numbers, billing address, or other financial information necessary to process payments for our services.
  • Project-related data: any specific information you provide about your project, such as website content, design preferences, or marketing goals, to help us deliver tailored solutions.
  • Technical data: information collected automatically when you visit our website, including your IP address, browser type, operating system, device type, pages visited, and the time and date of your visit. This data is collected via cookies, server logs, and analytics tools like Google Analytics.
  • Communication data: records of our correspondence with you, including emails, chat messages, or phone call notes, to ensure we can respond to your inquiries and provide support effectively.

We only collect data that is necessary for the purposes outlined in this policy. You are not obligated to provide any personal data, but failure to do so may limit our ability to provide certain services to you. For example, without your contact information, we may not be able to communicate with you about your project, and without payment details, we cannot process your order. We take care to minimize the data we collect and ensure that it is relevant to the services we provide.

IV. Purpose of Data Processing

Web-Impuls processes your personal data for the following purposes:

  • Service delivery: to fulfill your orders for web development, design, or digital promotion services, including creating websites, designing user interfaces, or managing SEO campaigns.
  • Communication: to respond to your inquiries, provide updates on your project, and send important notifications regarding our services or changes to our policies.
  • Website functionality: to ensure our website operates smoothly, including enabling features like contact forms, user accounts, and secure payment processing.
  • Analytics and improvement: to analyze user behavior on our website, track website performance, and improve our services based on user feedback and usage patterns.
  • Marketing: to send you promotional offers, newsletters, or updates about our services, but only if you have given your explicit consent to receive such communications. You can opt out at any time.
  • Legal compliance: to comply with legal obligations, such as tax regulations, anti-fraud measures, or responding to lawful requests from authorities.

We ensure that all data processing activities are based on a lawful basis, such as your consent, the necessity for contract fulfillment, or our legitimate interests, provided they do not override your rights and freedoms. For example, we may process your data to fulfill a contract (e.g., delivering a website you ordered), or we may use your email for marketing purposes if you have opted in to receive such communications. If you have any concerns about the purpose of our data processing, you can contact us for clarification.

V. Data Sharing

To provide our services, Web-Impuls may share your personal data with the following third parties:

  • Service providers: trusted partners who assist us in delivering our services, such as hosting providers (e.g., for website hosting), payment processors (e.g., for secure transactions), and analytics providers (e.g., Google Analytics for website traffic analysis).
  • Marketing partners: if you consent to marketing communications, we may share limited data with marketing platforms to deliver targeted advertisements or email campaigns.
  • Legal authorities: we may disclose your data if required by law, such as in response to a court order, subpoena, or other legal process, or to protect our rights, property, or safety.
  • Business transfers: in the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your data may be transferred to the new entity, but we will ensure your data remains protected under GDPR.

All third parties we share data with are required to comply with GDPR and other applicable data protection laws. We have agreements in place with these parties to ensure they process your data securely and only for the purposes specified by us. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties for their own marketing purposes. If we need to transfer your data outside the European Economic Area (EEA), we will ensure that appropriate safeguards, such as Standard Contractual Clauses, are in place to protect your data.

VI. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, as outlined in this Privacy Policy, or as required by law. Specific retention periods include:

  • Client data: personal and project-related data is retained for up to one year after the termination of your account or completion of your project, unless a longer period is required by law (e.g., for tax purposes).
  • Communication records: emails, chat messages, and other correspondence are kept for up to two years to ensure we can address any follow-up inquiries or disputes.
  • Technical data: data collected via cookies or analytics tools is retained for up to 12 months, after which it is anonymized or deleted.
  • Legal obligations: if required by law (e.g., for financial audits), some data, such as billing information, may be retained for up to 5 years or as mandated by local regulations.

Once the retention period expires, your data will be securely deleted or anonymized so that it can no longer be associated with you. If you request deletion of your data, we will comply with your request, subject to any legal obligations to retain certain information. For example, we may need to retain billing records for tax purposes even after you request deletion. We regularly review our data retention practices to ensure compliance with GDPR and other regulations.

VII. User Rights

As a data subject under GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to access: you can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you, including details about how it is processed.
  • Right to rectification: if your data is inaccurate or incomplete, you can request that we correct or update it.
  • Right to erasure: also known as the "right to be forgotten," you can request the deletion of your personal data, provided there are no legal grounds for retaining it.
  • Right to restrict processing: you can request that we limit the processing of your data in certain circumstances, such as if you contest its accuracy.
  • Right to data portability: you can request a copy of your data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and have it transferred to another controller.
  • Right to object: you can object to the processing of your data for specific purposes, such as direct marketing, or if the processing is based on our legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent: if we process your data based on your consent, you can withdraw that consent at any time, and we will cease processing.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details in the Contact Information section. We will respond to your request within 30 days, as required by GDPR. If your request is complex or involves a large volume of data, we may extend this period by an additional 60 days, but we will inform you of any such extension. If you believe your rights have been violated, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority, such as the Polish Data Protection Authority (UODO). We encourage you to contact us first so we can address your concerns directly.

VIII. Security Measures

Web-Impuls takes the security of your personal data seriously and has implemented a range of technical and organizational measures to protect it from unauthorized access, loss, alteration, or disclosure. These measures include:

  • Encryption: we use SSL/TLS encryption to secure data transmission between your device and our servers, ensuring that your data remains confidential during transit.
  • Access controls: we restrict access to your personal data to authorized personnel only, using strong authentication methods and role-based access controls.
  • Secure storage: our servers are hosted in secure data centers with physical and digital security measures, including firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and regular security audits.
  • Employee training: our staff are trained in data protection best practices and are required to follow strict confidentiality policies when handling your data.
  • Incident response: we have a comprehensive incident response plan in place to quickly address any data breaches or security incidents, including notifying affected users and authorities as required by GDPR.

Despite our efforts, no system can be completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute protection against all threats, such as sophisticated cyberattacks or unauthorized access by third parties. In the event of a data breach, we will notify you and the relevant authorities within 72 hours, as required by GDPR, and take immediate steps to mitigate any harm. We also encourage you to take steps to protect your data, such as using strong passwords and keeping your login credentials confidential.

IX. Cookies and Tracking

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience, analyze usage, and improve our services. Cookies are small text files stored on your device that help us understand how you interact with our website. We use the following types of cookies:

  • Essential cookies: these cookies are necessary for the basic functionality of our website, such as enabling navigation, accessing secure areas, and processing payments. You cannot opt out of these cookies as they are critical to the operation of our site.
  • Performance cookies: these cookies collect anonymized data about how you use our website, such as which pages you visit most often and if you encounter any errors. We use this data to improve our website's performance and user experience.
  • Functionality cookies: these cookies allow our website to remember your preferences, such as your language selection or display settings, to provide a more personalized experience.
  • Third-party cookies: we use third-party services like Google Analytics to analyze website traffic and user behavior. These cookies are set by external providers and are subject to their privacy policies.
  • Marketing cookies: if you consent, we may use cookies to deliver targeted advertisements or track the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns. These cookies may be set by us or by third-party advertising partners.

You can manage your cookie preferences through your browser settings or by using our cookie consent tool, which appears when you first visit our website. You can choose to accept or decline non-essential cookies, but disabling them may affect your experience on our site. For more details, please refer to our Cookie Policy, which provides additional information about the cookies we use and how to manage them. We are committed to being transparent about our use of cookies and ensuring that you have control over your data.
