What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites function efficiently, improve user experiences, and collect useful analytics. Cookies are essential in providing users with a personalized and seamless browsing experience.

Cookies can store information such as your preferences, login details, or the pages you visit. This information helps websites remember your settings and adapt their content to provide a smoother user experience.

How We Use Cookies

At Web-Impuls, we use cookies for several purposes, including:

  • Ensuring the proper functioning of our website's core features.
  • Providing a seamless and optimized browsing experience.
  • Storing user preferences, such as language settings or layout preferences.
  • Monitoring website performance to identify and resolve technical issues.
  • Analyzing website traffic to better understand our audience and improve our services.
  • Displaying targeted advertisements tailored to your interests.

Types of Cookies We Use

We classify the cookies we use into the following categories:

  • Essential Cookies: These cookies are required for the website to function properly and cannot be disabled.
  • Performance Cookies: These cookies collect anonymous data about how visitors use our site to improve its functionality.
  • Functional Cookies: These cookies remember your preferences and choices to enhance your browsing experience.
  • Advertising Cookies: These cookies are used to deliver relevant advertisements and measure their effectiveness.

Managing Your Cookies

Most modern web browsers allow you to control and manage cookies. You can delete cookies, block them, or set preferences for specific websites. Below are some common steps you can follow to manage your cookie settings:

  • Access your browser settings or preferences menu.
  • Find the "Privacy" or "Cookies" section.
  • Adjust your settings to block, delete, or allow cookies as needed.

Please note that disabling cookies may impact your ability to use some features of our website, such as remembering your preferences or saving your login details.

Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party cookies to provide additional features or services on our website. These cookies are set by external providers, such as analytics tools or social media platforms, and are governed by their respective privacy policies.

  • Google Analytics for monitoring site traffic and user behavior.
  • Social media plugins to enable content sharing or interactions.
  • Advertising networks to display relevant ads based on your preferences.

Updates to This Policy
